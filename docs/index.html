  • Key Terms
    • Each term in the list above is a key term of the early Wittgenstein's context principle.
  • Co-occurrence Windows (~1, ~2, ~3, ~4, ~5)
    • Each hyperlink associated with key terms (e.g. Zusammenhang) points to a page where the user can choose from 5 different co-occurrence windows.
    "~1" means that the window corresponds to a context of 1 word to the left and 1 word to the right of the target term.
    "~2" means that the window corresponds to a context of 2 words to the left and 2 words to the right of the target term.
    "~3" means that the window corresponds to a context of 3 words to the left and 3 words to the right of the target term.
    "~4" means that the window corresponds to a context of 4 words to the left and 4 words to the right of the target term.
    "~5" means that the window corresponds to a context of 5 words to the left and 5 words to the right of the target term.
  • Collocations
    • Each hyperlink associated with a co-occurrence window of a key term (e.g. Zusammenhang ~1) points to a page listing all collocations of that term for that co-occurrence window.
  • Remarks
    • Each hyperlink associated with a collocation points to a page listing all the remarks in which that collocation and the target term co-occur (according to the selected co-occurrence window). By clicking on one of the remark abbreviation, the user accesses the content of the remark in question (normalised version) in the Wittgenstein Source.
  • Wittgenstein Source
    • Wittgenstein Source is a website that is developed and run by the Wittgenstein Archives at the University of Bergen. It provides free access to Wittgenstein primary sources.
